WHAT IS A WRONGFUL DEATH CLAIM?

A death caused by the negligent or wrongful conduct of another person is actionable under the law. Any kind of accident that results in a fatality can be a wrongful death claim, though there are different timelines for giving notice and filing a claim. Additionally, depending on the type of accident and the status of the responsible party, different issues regarding liability may arise. The following types of accidents can result in wrongful death claims we can help with:

Passenger jet accidents

Air disasters in both private and commerical aviation are often fatal. We represent the loved ones of passengers killed in crashes involving commercial airliners, helicopters, air ambulances, medevac transports, commuter airlines, and private planes. Airplane disasters are often due to negligence of the pilot or maintenance crew.

Deaths during pregnancy, labor, and delivery, or in the 6 week period after delivery may be the fault of medical malpractice on behalf of the hospital or physician. Infant deaths are likewise often preventable, and tragic.

WRONGFUL DEATH ANSWERS

Can I file a wrongful death claim? How do you put a value on someone's life? Will it be enough to cover the expenses we've incurred? These are just some of the questions that the family members of wrongful death victims often ask. You deserve answers. And justice.

TEXAS STATE LAW

The law allows for the parents, spouses, and children of the decedent to bring a suit in a Texas court for damages caused by the wrongful death. Siblings of the decedent may not bring a claim for the loss of a brother or sister. A partner in a same-sex relationship is not permitted under Texas law to bring a wrongful death action. A survival claim may be brought by the decedent’s estate or heirs to recover for medical and funeral expenses and for any mental anguish the decedent experienced prior to the death. If the decedent died immediately, there is no claim for the decedent’s mental anguish.

FINANCIAL LOSS

The court will look into certain factors in determining you and your family's financial loss. This includes the earning potential of the decedent, his or her health, life expectancy, and assets. If the decedent was a housekeeper and did not have an outside job, you are still entitled to financial loss based on the value of the decedent’s role in childcare, housekeeping, and assistance with the family’s daily living needs. For your emotional loss, the jury or trier-of-fact will consider the strength of the relationship between you and the decedent. As your attorneys, we fight for you in court to make sure you are fully compensated.

DAMAGES

To prove financial loss, we often retain a financial expert to review the decedent’s employment records and history along with other factors to arrive at figure that values the compensation the decedent would have earned over his or her remaining working life. For emotional losses, we will review in depth with you the relationship you and other family members had with the decedent. We include family biographies, photographs, and testimonials. If exemplary damages are possible, such as in a drunk driving fatality or assault case, we will gather the evidence needed to support your exemplary damage claim.
